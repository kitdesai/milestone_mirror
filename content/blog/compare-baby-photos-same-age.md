Every parent with more than one child has had the moment: you're looking at your youngest and suddenly think, "they look exactly like their older sibling did at this age." You grab your phone, start scrolling through thousands of photos, and try to find the right one. By the time you do — if you do — the moment has passed.

## Why Compare Milestone Photos?

There's something uniquely moving about seeing your children at the same ages, side by side. It's not just about noticing who has whose eyes or smile. It's about seeing the thread of your family running through time.

Some parents discover that siblings who look nothing alike today were nearly identical at three months. Others find that each child brought a completely different energy to the same milestone — one cautious first-stepper, one fearless runner.

## The Problem with Photo Apps

Your camera roll organizes photos by date, not by age. Google Photos, iCloud, and every other photo app does the same. To compare your kids at three months old, you need to:

1. Remember roughly when each child turned three months
2. Scroll to that date for each child
3. Find a good photo from that period
4. Somehow view them side by side

This is tedious enough with two children. With three or more, it's nearly impossible.

Not sure where to start? Here are some popular milestone comparisons:

## Organize Your Story

Once you have a few frames, you can drag and drop them into any order. Some parents go chronological. Others group by theme — all the "firsts" together, or seasonal photos in a row.

The point isn't perfection. It's having a simple, beautiful way to see your family's story unfold — one milestone at a time.
